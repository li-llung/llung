Welcome to our June 2012 newsletter. This month, we invite you to explore the basics of AutoCAD and the foundations of programming, dive into real-world projects with Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator CS6, and learn to tune vocals like a pro with the music industry’s leading pitch-correction software.

CS6 training updates: Photoshop and Illustrator

In Photoshop CS6 for Photographers: Camera Raw 7, Chris Orwig reveals the improved processing power of Camera Raw 7, a tool that comes with Photoshop CS6. Chris shows how to open and manipulate raw format files with exceptional control over exposure and sharpness. In Illustrator CS6 One-on-One: Fundamentals, Deke McClelland walks you step by step through the core Illustrator features in the context of real-world projects.

 

New AutoCAD Essentials series

Given the vast scope of AutoCAD uses—from 2D and 3D CAD design to drafting and modeling to architectural drawing and engineering projects—we’ve broken our essential training into several pieces. The first three are available now: Interface and Drawing Management, Drawing Fundamentals, and Editing and Organizing Drawings. Watch for more installments in AutoCAD Essentials coming soon.

 

Vocal tuning with Melodyne

As the music industry’s leading pitch-correction software, Melodyne helps producers and engineers tune vocal performances. In Melodyne Essential Training, Emmy nominee and music editor Skye Lewin demonstrates the software’s tools, as well as workflows for using Melodyne products, and integrating them into your digital audio workstation. Find out how to play recorded vocals on a MIDI instrument, and edit polyphonic pitched recordings like changing one note within a prerecorded guitar chord.

 

Foundations of Programming: The next step

Our Foundations of Programming series began with a course that taught the core knowledge needed to begin programming in any language. Now author Simon Allardice continues with Foundations of Programming: Object-Oriented Design, covering the basic concepts you need to design a complex and coherent computer program. Among the recommended tips: Step away from the computer and pick up a stack of index cards to plan your program.

 

Invaluable training: Becoming a Leading Authority

The last course in Dave Crenshaw’s five-part career-improvement series shows how to identify areas where you can become a leader and decision maker, increasing your value within both your company and your community. (NOTE: The May newsletter mistakenly identified the fourth Invaluable course, Building Professional Connections, as the final installment.)

 

 

Until next time, happy learning!

—Lynda

Tip of the month

Every tool in the Photoshop toolbar can be chosen with a keyboard shortcut.

Switching tools using the keyboard from Photoshop CS6 Essential Training

Photoshop CS6 has a vast array of tools to help you create, improve, and alter your images. Switching between tools can be as simple as the press of a button, since every tool has a keyboard shortcut. For example, the Move tool’s keyboard shortcut is v, the Marquee is m, and the Lasso is l. If you don’t know the keyboard shortcut for a tool, simply hover over it and the tool tip will display the shortcut.

Some of the tools have additional tools nested beneath them, and you can identify these by the small triangle in the lower right-hand corner. To reveal the nested tools, just click on the tool and hold. All the other tools will then be displayed.

Keyboard shortcuts make switching between tools much quicker than clicking with the mouse. So if you type m, you’ll go to the Marquee tool. If you type l, you’ll go to the Lasso tool, and so on. But what if you want to access one of the nested tools using a keyboard shortcut? Hold down the Shift key, and then the shortcut key. Pressing Shift+l will cycle through all the Lasso tools. If you prefer to toggle through the tools without using the Shift key, you can change that in Preferences. On a Mac, it’s under Preferences > General, and on Windows, you’ll find it under Edit > Preferences > General. Then just uncheck the option Use Shift Key for Tool Switch. With that option unchecked, you can just tap the shortcut key to cycle through the different tools.

Using the keyboard shortcuts to switch between tools is a great time-saver. Don’t worry about learning the shortcuts for all of the tools right away. Eventually, you’ll learn which you need, and soon using them will be second nature.

If you’re interested in more Photoshop tips, check out Julieanne Kost’s full course, Photoshop CS6 Essential Training.
